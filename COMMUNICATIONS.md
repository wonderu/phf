# Communications checklist (PHF)

Checklist for project communications — inside the team and outside (stakeholders, customers, vendors). Use during handover to capture cadence, owners, and links.

## Communications inside the team

- [ ] **Standups / daily sync**
	- Cadence (daily / every other day / per team)
	- Time, timezone, duration
	- Format (in-person, async, video, chat)
	- Owner/facilitator
	- Template or prompts (e.g. What did you do? What will you do? Blockers?)
	- Where notes or updates are stored (if any)
	- [Atlassian: What is a stand up meeting & tips to run one](https://www.atlassian.com/agile/scrum/standups)

- [ ] **Sprint / iteration planning**
	- Cadence and duration
	- Who attends, who runs it
	- Agenda and artifacts (backlog, capacity, goals)
	- Link to board or planning doc
	- [Atlassian: Sprint planning](https://www.atlassian.com/agile/scrum/sprint-planning)

- [ ] **Retrospectives**
	- Cadence (e.g. end of sprint, bi-weekly, monthly)
	- Facilitator and format (e.g. Start/Stop/Continue, Mad/Sad/Glad)
	- Where outcomes and action items are recorded
	- [Atlassian: Retrospectives](https://www.atlassian.com/team-playbook/plays/retrospective) · [Retromat](https://retromat.org/)

- [ ] **Refinement / backlog grooming**
	- Cadence and owner
	- Who participates (dev, QA, PM, etc.)
	- Definition of Ready / DoD reference
	- [Atlassian: Backlog refinement](https://www.atlassian.com/agile/scrum/backlog-refinement)

- [ ] **Tech / architecture sync**
	- Cadence (e.g. weekly tech sync, ad-hoc design reviews)
	- Who runs it, who attends
	- Where decisions and ADRs are documented
	- [Documenting architecture decisions (ADR)](https://adr.github.io/)

- [ ] **Team all-hands / team meeting**
	- Cadence and duration
	- Agenda (wins, priorities, announcements, Q&A)
	- Owner and where notes live

- [ ] **1:1s**
	- Cadence per role (e.g. PM–dev, PM–stakeholder)
	- Who drives agenda, where follow-ups are tracked
	- [Atlassian: One-on-one meetings](https://www.atlassian.com/blog/inside-atlassian/1-on-1-meeting-tips)

## Communications outside the team

- [ ] **Status reports**
	- Cadence (weekly / bi-weekly / monthly)
	- Audience (sponsor, steering, customers)
	- Template and sections (summary, progress, risks, next period)
	- Owner and distribution (email, Confluence, dashboard)
	

- [ ] **Steering committee / sponsor updates**
	- Cadence and duration
	- Attendees and chair
	- Standard agenda and deck template
	- Where decisions and minutes are stored

- [ ] **Stakeholder updates**
	- Per-stakeholder or per-group cadence
	- Preferred channel (email, Slack, meeting)
	- Key contacts and escalation path
	- Link to stakeholder register or RACI

- [ ] **Customer / client communication**
	- Regular touchpoints (e.g. weekly sync, monthly business review)
	- Owner (PM, account manager, tech lead)
	- Where customer feedback and requests are logged
	- Contract or SLA communication (notices, change requests)

- [ ] **Vendor / outstaff communication**
	- Cadence with each vendor or outstaff lead
	- Escalation and issue reporting
	- Link to contracts and contacts (see [13. Contract and Budget](README.md#-13-contract-and-budget))

- [ ] **Escalation path**
	- When and how to escalate (blockers, risks, scope)
	- Who is notified at each level
	- Documented in project charter or wiki

## Channels and tools

- [ ] **Meeting and video**
	- Default tool (Zoom, Meet, Teams, etc.)
	- Recurring links and how to find them
	- Recording and retention policy

- [ ] **Chat / instant messaging**
	- Primary channel (Slack, Teams, etc.)
	- Key channels or groups (project, #incidents, #releases)
	- Expectations (response time, after hours)

- [ ] **Email**
	- When email is used vs chat (e.g. formal decisions, external)
	- Mailing lists or distribution groups for status

- [ ] **Documentation and async updates**
	- Where meeting notes, status reports, and retros live (Confluence, Notion, SharePoint, repo wiki)
	- Naming and template conventions
	- Link to [9. Documentation](README.md#-9-documentation)

## Team contact details

Capture and hand over how to reach each team member (and key stakeholders) for day-to-day and escalation. Prefer a single source (e.g. team wiki, HR directory) and keep it access-controlled.

- [ ] **Emails**
	- Work emails for all team members and key stakeholders
	- Mailing lists or distribution groups (e.g. team@, dev@, oncall@)
	- When to use email vs chat (e.g. formal decisions, external parties)

- [ ] **Mobiles**
	- Work numbers (who has a work phone, who to call for incidents)
	- Private numbers (only where explicitly shared for handover/on-call; respect privacy and local policies)
	- On-call rotation and how to find the current on-call number

- [ ] **Telegram (or other messengers)**
	- Who uses Telegram (or Slack, WhatsApp, etc.) for project/urgent contact
	- Project or group links/handles (e.g. team group, incident channel)
	- Expectations: response time, after hours, when to use vs email/phone
